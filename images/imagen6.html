TGM Bread
 
 
 
 

In January 2016, three years after opening The General Muir at Emory Point, we opened TGM Bread next door. We originally made all the bread for The General Muir — hand rolled kettle boiled bagels, ciabatta for sandwiches, buns for burgers, brioche for French toast, pletzel bread, and, of course, rye — out of the small pastry kitchen in the restaurant. As we added restaurants, we outgrew that space (and sharing it with our growing pastry squad was getting tricky!) and had the wonderful opportunity to take over the space immediately next door.

While we are open to the public for only limited hours for TGM Soup Co. and retail bread purchases, most hours of the day you can peek in the window and see someone making bread and enjoy the delicious smells as you pass by.

Our pastry department at The General Muir is responsible for the other baked goodies you know and love -- layer cakes, cheesecakes, cookies, croissants, danish, cinnamon rolls, coffee cakes, babka, and more! Visit the Deli counter at The General Muir to see what we baked that day or reach out about a special order.

wholesale bakery

TGM Bread bakes all the bread for the Rye Restaurants (the restaurant group name a nod to the common thread) including The General Muir, Fred's Meat & Bread, Yalla, Wood’s Chapel BBQ, Dirty Rascal, and West Egg, and many other restaurants around Atlanta (see below). We’re proud to know that some of the best sandwiches in Atlanta — whether in a restaurant or your home — are served on TGM Bread. Besides being a tangible link between all of our restaurants, the idea of “breaking bread” with a friend, family, neighbor, or stranger is hospitality at its core.
